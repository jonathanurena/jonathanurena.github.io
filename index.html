The Problem

Debt doesn't start with recklessness.
It starts with life.

A medical bill arrives. A job disappears. A baby comes before the savings do. Debt doesn't announce itself. It just shows up — and quietly takes over.

Most people aren't careless. They're just carrying more than anyone sees. And the tools that promised to help? They either overwhelm with complexity people don't trust, or quietly turn around and sell the very habits they were hired to fix.

The people who need the most help are being let down by the very thing holding their hand.

"What if someone finally built a tool that was completely, unconditionally on the user's side?"

The Idea

We built Fiinly for the person who's tired of being afraid to look at their bank account.

Your data stays with you. We never see it. We never sell it. We never profit from the cycle we're helping you break.

Ten seconds to log a purchase. Five minutes to see your whole picture. And for the first time — a real date. The exact day your debt is gone, if you follow your plan. Not a guess. Not a projection. A date you can circle. Count down to. Hold onto.

Debt stops feeling like weather. It starts feeling like something you can beat.

Our Promise

Privacy isn't a feature.
It's the foundation.

Most financial apps exist inside a larger machine. Your spending habits have value — to advertisers, to partners, to people you'll never meet. That's not a bug. It's the model. And it works against you.

Fiinly is different. We earn when you pay for the app. Nothing more. Your data never leaves your device. We don't know what you spend. We don't want to. The moment we profit from your habits, we stop being on your side.

We'd rather be the most trusted tool in your pocket than the most profitable one in ours.

Built for Real Life

Money is rarely just one person's problem.

A couple sits at a kitchen table. One knows what was spent. The other doesn't. That moment — the tension, the silence — it's one of the most common sources of stress in a home.

Fiinly integrates with Apple Family Sharing so households move together. Same budget. Same goals. Same page. When a family stops guessing and starts seeing — something quietly changes. The table gets a little quieter. In the best possible way.

Financial stress doesn't stay in the spreadsheet. It follows people to bed. It sits with them at dinner. It shows up in arguments that were never really about money — but always were.
